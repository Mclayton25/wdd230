Visual Hierarchy

Samsung Homepage

Samsung.com

Visual Hierarchy is a principle that artists and designers use to know how to structure visual characteristics. This principle helps the user easily interprit the inormation that is being portrayed. Visual Hierarchy items: Size, Color, Contrast, Alignment, Repetition, Proximity, Whitespace, Texture and Color

Samsung takes their front page and takes the users eyes so they focus on their most popular items. They show phones, tvs, and other tech and then make it easy for the user to go to the shop and buy what they would like.

White Space and Clean Design

Google Pixel 7 Pro

Google.com

This page follows great White Space rules and clean design. The phone is the prominent item being shown, but the space in the backround that matches the phone color is well pronounced but not overpowering.

Each attribute in this page is important and pronounced but not one of them overtakes the main subject. The way the page keeps the users eyes focused on the phone but keeps everything easy to understand and find is clean and sharp.

Alignment

W3 Schools

W3schools.com

Good page element allignment shows organization and does not distract or lose the user.

The items are shown in an orderly manor, it is easy to navigate. The home and next buttons are easy to follow. The information provided in the bottom with the HTML tags is well defined and in a way that the user knows how to understand the information provided.
